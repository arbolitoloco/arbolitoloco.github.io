Hi! I'm Laura,

a crafty scientist who loves to code.

I'm an entomologist, based in São Paulo, Brazil, specialized in the evolution and classification of neotropical leaf beetles. On my current full-time job as a Museum Research Technician, I'm responsible for supervising and training new contractors that do data-entry in a biodiversity database, which gathers information about insects from a Natural History Museum.

I've always been passionate about programming, having written my first lines of code as a child. A few years ago I started freelancing as a front end web developer, as a side job, coding everything from responsive newsletters, to one-page websites and custom Wordpress themes for e-commerces.

I like to imagine and make things, and that is also reflected in my hobbies, which include arts & crafts. During my graduate studies, I started a small brand of handcrafted items called Fina Paca.

I love the interface between science, creativity, and technology, and I'm hoping I can find a career in that multidisciplinary area. I support open science, and I try to encourage the use of technology tools in Taxonomy. I am also active in promoting science communication, and offer support to fellow women scientists in Brazil, through a community-driven website that I developed and manage (with the help of a team of editors) called Mulheres na Ciência.
